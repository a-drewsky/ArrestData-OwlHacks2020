Sphere of Incarceration

Sphere of influence is a term used to describe the power a country has over neighboring countries. In much the same way, incarceration impacts much more than just the arrestee. When someone is incarcerated and cannot post bail it has a significant impact on that person's friends and family. In this simulation, we assume a normal distribution of data where family size is based on census data, and each person has a normal distribution of friends with an assumed mean of 5 friends. When someone is arrested, it negatively influences family members and impacts their friends albeit to a lesser degree.

The effects are visualized by the level of red coloration. The more negative influence a person is experiencing the more it slows down their progress. This simulation shows just how many people are negatively impacted when someone is arrested and is unable to post bail.

(200-4000, large number might take longer to load)

This simulation is based on census data and arrest data for The City of Philadelphia in the year 2020. Data was provided by Philly Bail Fund. The number of records used is scaled, based on the selected population size. Since this is a scaled down representation of the Philadelphia population, there is a correlated number of records used which are chosen at random.

The simulation starts at the beginning of 2020, each time an arrest was made where the arrestee was unable to post bail, a person in the simulation is set as arrested and their level of negative influence is set to 100%. For 100 days after the arrest, the level of negative influence for each family member is increase at a decreasing rate, resulting in about a 50% increase in negative influence over that time. The level of negative influence for each member of the population is set to be no less than half of whichever friend has the most negative influence.
